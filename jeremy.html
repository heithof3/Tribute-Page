SGT Jeremy Robbins:

While on a Medical convoy Jeremy’s unit was ambushed. The scary thing was the snippers were firing antitank rounds. One hit the hummer passenger door (most vulnerable spot). The round penetrated & hit his Sargent first before ricocheting hitting the driver, Jeremy’s good friend. The main fragment hit the right side of this head blowing out much of his brain. While under fire Jeremy disembarked & pulled his friend out of the driver seat where he was severely stuck. The pull caused him to fall backwards (both with full metal plates on) with his friend landing on top of him. He was dying, blood everywhere, but while under fire he got his buddy in the follow-up medical van. (Earning him only the Combat Pin). He made the command decision not to call for helicopter evat- support but to run through the ambush & return to the hospital where his friend was saved. Sadly, for the rest of his life his buddy will have to wear a helmet to protect his damaged head. Upon Company deactivation his body’s parents found Jeremy & hugged him for several minutes crying in thanks.
